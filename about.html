About the University

Chaudhary Devi Lal University (CDLU) is a government state university located in Sirsa, Haryana, India. Named after the former Deputy Prime Minister of India and former Chief Minister of Haryana, Jan Nayak Chaudhary Devi Lal, the university was established by the Government of Haryana under Act 9 of 2003 passed by the State Legislature on 2 April 2003.

The university originated as Kurukshetra University's Chaudhary Devi Lal Post Graduate Regional Centre Sirsa, set up in 2000. In April 2003, it was declared a full-fledged university. CDLU is recognized by the University Grants Commission (UGC) and declared eligible for Central Assistance under Section 12(B) of the UGC Act.

Spread across a sprawling 213-acre campus on Barnala Road, Sirsa, the university boasts four teaching blocks, the Vivekananda Library, five well-furnished hostels, a Media Centre with Community Radio Station, a majestic Multi-Purpose Hall, a shopping complex, a university health centre, and 144 residential houses for staff.

Our History

Tracing the journey of CDLU from a regional centre to a full-fledged university.

2000

Regional Centre Established

Kurukshetra University established the Chaudhary Devi Lal Post Graduate Regional Centre in Sirsa to serve the educational needs of the region.

2003

University Status Granted

Declared a full-fledged university on 2 April 2003 under Haryana Act No. 9 of 2003, named after Jan Nayak Chaudhary Devi Lal.

2009

UGC Recognition under 12(B)

Recognized by the University Grants Commission and declared eligible for Central Assistance, opening doors for research funding and development.

2015

Expansion Phase

Construction of Teaching Block III, Girls Hostel No. 3, and multiple residential buildings. Wi-Fi enabled campus launched.

2024

Teaching Block IV Inaugurated

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam Bhawan (Teaching Block IV) inaugurated along with new centres including UCOPE and Centre for Rural Studies.
